Understanding My 2016 Writing Style

To explain this post, this post goes into depth with regard to how I wrote back in 2016. I was not satisfied with modernizing and formalizing my writing, because I felt that it ruined the entire feel of the story and how I wrote it. I had a particular style, and my perception has changed regarding older works I've made, whether stories or games. For a long while, I believed that I could modernize my older games and stories, but I see now the value in their cultural characteristics, style, and accompanying limitations.

Contributing to this was the incompatibility I experienced while attempting to modernize my older writings with my now-outdated framework, which I view now as rational and objectivist without making room for the pluralist vastness and richness of the past. So this post represents my desire to be more well-rounded in my assessment and continuation of the past, not that of rational, objectivist, presentist removal, but that of respecting the past.

Guideline:

  1. The author is fine with using sentences where the direct quotation comes before the dialogue tag like in the following.

    "It's time! Ahh." I said to myself gladly while stretching my back.

  2. The author does not use comma with direct quotations as observed in the above-stated sentence example.

  3. The author prefers using present participles in adverbial phrases as seen in the following.

    Before leaving,

  4. The author groups can be observed grouping scenes in one paragraph, including dialogue and actions by different characters, such as the foregoing narrative excerpt.

  5. The author has been seen putting comma outside of the direct quotation marks to mark the dialogue tag even if there is already an ending punctuation inside the quotation marks. This is seen in the following.

    "It's been really good having you here bro!", Josh said,

    "God bless you bro, you totally were a miracle to my adventurer life…Don't worry, I was fine even without you.", Josh mocked me.

    "Let's meetup again, someday, somehow.", I told him gladly.

    However, he also is not very strict with this preference, as seen in the following.

    "It's time! Ahh." I said to myself gladly while stretching my back.

    In addition, the rule 5 placement is not limited to commas, as he has used periods as well, even after a period inside the quotes. This is observed in the following sentence from the excerpt.

    ...Peter declined, "No, what do we do?".

    ...David agreed, "Right!".

    ..."But, that was about a month ago, about the time Janessa first arrived here.".

    To add to this, the author has done the comma placement of Rule 5 even without a dialogue tag. This is evidenced by the following.

    Not the best way to wake up, so I pushed "horsey" away so I could stand up. "Hey Sir Jones, thanks for the stay.", I was about to leave until my kind instinct kicked in.

Other much more minor style rules include the following.

  1. Words being cut off partially as opposed to being cut off after the full word is spelled, with a hyphen instead of an em dash.

    ..."Hey guys, ea-"...

    ..."A new quest, Missi-"...

  2. The use of "?!"

    "HEY! I felt someone think I was crazy! Who is it?!"...

  3. All-caps for emphasis.

    EHHHHHHHHHHHHHHHH

  4. Repetition of letters

    David's face was like, "GEMMSSSS!". "Okay guys, time to sleepppppppp!", I sang.

  5. The spelling out of a whole phrase as a hyphenated adjective

    ...please-don't-wake-me-up morning routine...

  6. The use of "like" to denote indirect speech that is styled as if direct and without capitalization

    I'm guessing Christie was like "boys, so weird."

  7. The use of "--" or "---" to denote an em dash or ellipsis

    "We were umm--- gonna tell you about Timothy's first mission for Rank 3, but you don't have to go."...

    "No, its fine, I'm fine--*silence*--what's the quest's requests?".

  8. The use of onomatopoeia (formatted as *THUD* with asterisks)
